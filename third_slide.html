Fuel Efficiency

On key technological innovation that came to maturation in the 1980s was turbocharging. By using the pressure of the engine's exhaust to spin a turbine, the turbocharger forces more of the air-fuel mixture into the engine, increasing power. On the graph to the left, turbocharged cars have higher horsepower than normally aspirated cars at the same MPG. Diesel cars have better fuel efficiency, but need turbocharging to have adequate power.
